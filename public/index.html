HALFWAY DOWN

A manifesto on men's mental health by Daan Matheeuwsen

  • Destigmatization
  • Community Support
  • Accessibility

I firmly believe that the path to healing begins with the courage to open up, share experiences, and reject the outdated notion that vulnerability is synonymous with weakness. I redefine strength as the courage to be vulnerable. Men, like all individuals, should not bear the weight of societal expectations that discourage the expression of emotions. True strength lies in acknowledging one's struggles, seeking help, and navigating the path to well-being with resilience and authenticity. In our commitment to destigmatization, I recognize that each person's journey is unique. Our manifesto is a call to action, inviting individuals, communities, and society at large to embrace vulnerability, foster understanding, and join hands in rewriting the narrative around men's mental health. Together, I will break down the walls of silence and build a world where mental well-being is a shared priority, free from judgment and stigma.

SPEAK UP

As I draw the curtains on this manifesto, I implore you to join me in the collective effort to Speak up, Promote, Empathize, Advocate, and Kindle hope for men's mental health. It is not merely a manifesto; it is a rallying cry for change, a testament to our shared commitment to transform the landscape of men's mental well-being.

Stand Together
Together, we stand against the shadows of stigma and silence. Our unity is a powerful force, breaking down the barriers that perpetuate the isolation felt by many men navigating the complexities of mental health.

Promote Open Dialogue
We promote open dialogue, where every voice matters. By encouraging conversations, we create a space where men can share their struggles, victories, and everything in between, fostering an environment of understanding and compassion.

Empathize and Support
Let empathy guide our actions. Understanding the unique challenges faced by men in their mental health journeys, we extend a hand of support, dismantling stereotypes and fostering a culture where vulnerability is met with compassion.
Advocate for Change
Advocacy becomes our weapon against the status quo. We challenge outdated norms, destigmatize seeking help, and demand a society that embraces mental well-being as an integral part of human experience.

Kindle Hope
In our collective efforts, we kindle hope. We recognize that behind every statistic, there are real lives—individuals with dreams, struggles, and the inherent right to a hopeful and fulfilling life.

Sustain Lifelong Learning
Recognizing that mental well-being is an evolving journey, I emphasize the importance of continual education about mental health. By fostering a culture of curiosity and ongoing learning, we empower individuals to understand their mental health needs at different stages of life.

Speaking in Numbers

Global Suicide Rates:
Men are three times more likely to die by suicide than women globally. In 2020, the global suicide rate among men was approximately 13.7 per 100,000 individuals, significantly higher than the rate for women, which was around 4.8 per 100,000.

Age-Specific Data:
Among men, suicide rates tend to be highest in older age groups. For example, in many countries, the highest rates are observed in men aged 45-64.

Societal Impact:
Suicide is a leading cause of death among men globally, impacting not only individuals but families, communities, and societies at large.

Barriers to Seeking Help:
Stigma and societal expectations around masculinity often act as barriers to men seeking mental health support, contributing to a higher risk of untreated mental health issues.

Impact of Mental Health Disorders:
Mental health disorders, if left untreated, can significantly impact a man's overall well-being, affecting relationships, work, and daily functioning.

Call to Action:
These numbers are not mere statistics; they represent fathers, sons, brothers, and friends. Each life lost to suicide is a profound tragedy, underscoring the urgent need for a collective commitment to change.

In Conclusion
To sum up, this manifesto is a powerful call to action—a shared commitment to rewrite the history of men's mental health. We discover a common dedication to change as we navigate the concepts of destigmatization, community support, accessibility, intersectionality, ongoing learning, and the strength of SPEAKing UP. We recognize the gravity of our purpose as we face the brutal truth of numbers and the fact that every figure represents a person, a narrative, and a chance for change. SPEAK Up serves as our rallying cry, encouraging us to Stand, Promote, Empathize, Advocate, Kindle Hope, and Sustain lifetime learning. The journey ahead is not alone; rather, it is a united movement. Maintaining mental health is a lifetime commitment rather than a destination. Will you join me in the SPEAK up movementy? Our voice becomes the driving force behind revolutionary change, creating a future in which every person—male or female—walks a path lighted by compassion, acceptance, and the steadfast support of a single, supportive community. Are you going to talk to me? We have a road ahead of us, one in which our voices will serve as a catalyst for revolutionary change regarding men's mental health. By working together, we can rewrite the story and create a society in which mental health is unaffected by silence.
